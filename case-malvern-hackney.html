(01) — The brief

A four-storey Victorian house, rebuilt around its bones.

A returning private client asked for a complete refurbishment of their Hackney home — four storeys, Victorian, with original parquet and lime plaster worth saving. The brief was a working family house, not a show-piece.

We led the project under a single D&B contract, taking the house back to brick where needed and consolidating where it was already right. Heritage consents, party walls and neighbour relations were all handled in-house.

(02) — KitchenMarble + oak

A kitchen built as furniture

The kitchen is solid oak with a single book-matched marble run. We coordinated the slab, drew the joinery to the veins, and trial-fitted the run before site delivery. The island carries a 3m unsupported overhang detailed by our workshop.

(03) — HeritageParquet + plaster

What we kept, and how

Original parquet on the ground floor was lifted, repaired and relaid. Three sections of lime plaster cornice were consolidated rather than replaced; new lime was scribed to meet the original line. Sash windows were drawn back to their early profile and re-glazed with slim-line units.

Parquet · lifted & relaid Lime plaster patched Sash windows · re-drawn Slim-line double glazing Original chimney breast Cast iron rads · refurbished
(04) — Programme42 weeks total

Decanted, not endured

The client moved out at the start. We programmed structural work and basement first, kept the house weather-tight through the wet trades, and stacked finishes from top down so the family could move back into a ready house, not a snag list.
