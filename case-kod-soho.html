(01) — The brief

A listed Soho townhouse, rebuilt for service.

The brief was direct — turn a tired Soho frontage into a working restaurant for a returning operator. Listed building consent, period fabric to preserve, neighbours on three sides, and a hard date to open against a marketing programme already in motion.

We led the project from RIBA Stage 2 in-house: heritage approvals, joinery, finishes and M&E coordination. The result is a room that reads as if it has always been there — and a kitchen and pass that run at full hospitality tempo.

(02) — JoineryIn-house workshop

A bar built in our workshop

The bar front is solid walnut, glued up and machined at our Bexley workshop, then hand-finished on site. The brass top rail and toe-kick were drawn full-size against the listed cornice to sit inside the existing rhythm of the room.

(03) — HeritageGrade II listed

Working around the listing

The ceiling cornice, two original chimney breasts and a section of panelled wall were retained and consolidated rather than replaced. Listed Building Consent was led in-house, with the Westminster conservation officer attending three site visits across the programme.

LBC — Westminster Cornice consolidation Original chimney breast Lime plaster — patched Sash window repair Floorboards — lifted & relaid
(04) — Programme22 weeks total

Phased to a hard opening date

Marketing was in market eight weeks before practical completion. We back-built from the date, sequencing M&E first-fix to free up the heritage fabric for late-programme consolidation. The kitchen handed over a week clear of the soft launch.
